Identiscape is a web and mobile phone-based social networking platform for user-created 360 cinematic VR landscapes that reflect the identities and cultures of VR makers.




Design Questions

- How are people’s identities shaped by their environment?
- How are our memories tied to places we visit or places in which we live?
- How does the landscape tie our memories and the concept of self?
- How can I create a comfortable user experience for posting personal 360 videos?
- How can I make cinematic VR more accessible to people without access to expensive
head-mounted display technology?

The Connection Between the Landscape and Identity


The point where the landscape and identity comingle is where when an individual is connecting with their surroundings. For J.M.W. Turner, it was during his travels (the landscape) that he saw the light of God (identity—religion) and where he was able to tie his experiences in his paintings. The moment that I feel most connected to my identity is when I am around family (culture). For family gatherings, we drive hundreds of miles in and around the tri-state area and Mid-Atlantic coast to sit around an elongated table at my home to converse about our lives, and eat. The family gatherings that we have and the people who surround me are of utmost importance, and what in part comprises my identity. The 360 videos below consist of some of these moments:









Prescedents

Google Immerse VR videos investigate race, diversity, and identity by exploring how real people interact with the world and how the world interacts with them.

Since not everyone has the privilege of owning a VR headset (for example, to watch Dezzie’s video) and, thus, usable to participate in this VR experience. Alternatively, our only choice would be to watch the videos through YouTube or Vimeo. But, of course, these would not be in VR. Accordingly, to make VR or 360 cinematic video more accessible, there needs to be another option. Using a website (platform) to show 360 videos, may be the way to go. What would make my platform different from YouTube is that one could explore videos like Dezzie’s, and conveniently find them in one place? The user would be able to search by identity or location (landscape) and watch a 360 video.
