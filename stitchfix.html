Stitch Fix Collaboration

Project | UX Design & Human/AI Interactions
My Role | Experience Design, User Research, Concept Development, and Prototyping
Tools | IBM Watson, Sketch, Illustrator, Photoshop, InVision
Project for | MFADT Spring 2017 Interface Cognitive AI and IBM Watson Collaboration
Team Members | Danielle Sobel
Year & Place | 2017 Parsons School of Design


The aim for this project was to make shopping with the application Stitch Fix easy and enjoyable.
By pairing Artificial Intelligence and IBM Watson with a human element, personal styling, customers will recieve an effortless shopping experience. With this Watson solution designed for a business, such as Stitch Fix, customers will have a short conversation with a chat bot, so stylists can get a better understanding of what the customer likes. This will leave the customer feeling good, and not so frustrated by wasting time.




Benefits

According to IBM, conversation chat bots can reduce customer care costs by up to 29%.

Watson Chat Bot Prototype




Design Process

Stitch Fix learns about their users using a style quiz. This quiz takes up to 15 minutes to learn about each user. I tried to challenge this aspect by thinking how AI could learn much more quickly about personal style compared to doing the quiz yourself.
